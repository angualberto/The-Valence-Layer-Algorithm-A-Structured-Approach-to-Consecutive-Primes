Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript entitled

  "The Valence Layer Algorithm:
   Prime Gaps, Phase Symmetry, and the Riemann Hypothesis"

for publication in the Journal of Integer Sequences.

The paper presents the following integer sequences and their properties:

  (1) The Valence Toolbox, an integer sequence
      V = {1000, 500, 200, 120, 64, 34, 32, 30, 28, 24, 20, 18,
           16, 12, 10, 8, 6, 4, 2}
      that exactly decomposes every prime gap G > 1 by a greedy
      algorithm.  This is a new finite integer sequence with
      algorithmic applications.

  (2) The Liouville function lambda(n) = (-1)^{Omega(n)} and its
      partial sums L(N) up to N = 10^8.  The ratio
      max|L(N)|/sqrt(N) stabilises at approximately 1.33 across
      three orders of magnitude.  We provide the full computed
      sequence and analyse its growth.

  (3) The phase-symmetry condition K(s)K(1-s) in R for
      K(s) = 2^s - 1, which is algebraically equivalent to
      Re(s) = 1/2 and extends to any base q > 0 via
      K_q(s) = q^s - 1.

  (4) Goldbach partition counts for even numbers up to 10^7,
      following the Hardy--Littlewood asymptotic.
